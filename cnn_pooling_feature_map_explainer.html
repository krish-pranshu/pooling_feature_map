How CNNs See: Feature Maps & Pooling

Watch how an image is scanned by a "Filter" to create a Feature Map, and then how it gets summarized (shrunk) through Max Pooling.

What is happening?

  • 1
    Scanning (Convolution): A small sliding window (the yellow box) moves across the original image. It's looking for a specific pattern. When it finds a strong match, it records a high number in the new Feature Map.
  • 2
    Summarizing (Max Pooling): Now, we take the Feature Map and look at it in 2x2 blocks (the red box). For each block, we only keep the largest number. This shrinks our map down while keeping the most important "loudest" signals.
